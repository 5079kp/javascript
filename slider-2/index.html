Cherry Blossom

Cherry blossoms, or sakura in Japanese, are the flowers of cherry trees, primarily from the Prunus genus. They are renowned for their delicate beauty and are deeply symbolic in Japanese culture, representing both the fleeting nature of life and the beauty of spring. These trees are not typically grown for their fruit, but rather for their ornamental value and the spectacular blooms they produce each spring.

Plumeria Rubra

Plumeria rubra is a deciduous plant species belonging to the genus Plumeria. Commonly known as frangipani, it is a deciduous, semi-succulent shrub or small tree known for its fragrant, colorful flowers and attractive foliage. Native to Mexico and Central America but widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide. Oblong to elliptical, leathery, and glossy dark green leaves are arranged alternately along the branches. Ornamental: Widely planted in gardens, parks, and landscapes for its beauty and fragrance.

WaterLily

Water lilies are aquatic plants with large, flat, floating leaves called lily pads and showy, often fragrant flowers that rise to the water's surface. They are rooted in the soil at the bottom of ponds, lakes, or slow-moving streams, and they thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments like ponds, lakes, and slow-flowing streams. Some species bloom during the day, while others bloom at night.

BlueBell

Bell-shaped, usually violet-blue, but can be pink or white. They have six petals that are often curled back at the tips. Narrow, strap-shaped, and green, growing from the base of the plant. English bluebells have a sweet, often strong scent, while Spanish bluebells have little to no scent. Bluebell bulbs have been traditionally used for medicinal purposes, including diuretic and styptic properties. Hybridization with Spanish bluebells can threaten the native bluebell population.

Rose

Roses are popular flowering shrubs known for their beauty and fragrance. They come in various colors and sizes, with thorns on their stems and fragrant, multi-petaled flowers. Roses are also known for their cultural significance, symbolizing love, beauty, and various emotions. Roses are adaptable to various climates and can be grown as shrubs, climbers, or ground cover.
